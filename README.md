# Nepal Newar Ceremonial Metalwork Registry

This registry preserves and authenticates ceremonial metalworking knowledge from the Newar communities of the Kathmandu Valley.  
Newar metalwork is one of the oldest continuously practiced ritual crafts in Asia, and its techniques—transmitted through guilds, families, and temple workshops—are at risk of disappearing as traditional artisans decline.

## Purpose

The purpose of this contract is to create a permanent, tamper‑resistant record of verified ceremonial metalworking practices.  
By storing these techniques on-chain, the registry ensures that the cultural memory of Newar ritual craftsmanship cannot be lost, altered, or misrepresented over time.

## Why this contract is necessary

Traditional Newar metalwork is more than a craft: it is a ritual discipline tied to temples, festivals, and consecration ceremonies.  
However, the knowledge behind these practices is vulnerable:

- Many workshops rely on oral transmission.
- Younger generations are leaving the craft.
- Global commercialization often detaches objects from their cultural meaning.
- Documentation is scattered, inconsistent, or inaccessible.

An on-chain registry solves these issues by:

- Providing a **neutral, immutable record** of authentic techniques.
- Allowing communities and researchers to **submit verified information**.
- Preventing the **loss or distortion** of ritual knowledge.
- Offering a **public, transparent reference** for future generations.

## What makes this contract special

Unlike generic cultural registries, this contract focuses specifically on **ritual metalwork**, not general craftsmanship.  
It captures the intersection of:

- technique  
- cultural meaning  
- ceremonial function  

This combination reflects how Newar artisans understand their work:  
**a technical process with spiritual purpose**.

The registry is intentionally minimalistic.  
It does not attempt to catalogue every detail—only the essential elements needed to preserve authenticity and context.

## How it works

Anyone can record a technique, but each entry must include:

- the region or workshop lineage  
- the community or neighborhood  
- the name of the technique  
- the material used  
- a cultural or historical note  
- its ritual function  

The contract also includes a simple like/dislike system to allow community feedback and visibility.

## Long-term vision

This registry aims to become a permanent cultural anchor for Newar ritual metalwork—  
a reference point for artisans, researchers, museums, and future generations seeking accurate, respectful documentation of these practices.

By placing this knowledge on-chain, the registry ensures that the legacy of Newar ceremonial metalwork remains accessible, protected, and preserved beyond the limits of physical archives.
